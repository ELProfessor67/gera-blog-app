Harvard Innovation Labs at the Harvard Business School, together with Amazon Web Services (AWS), are co-sponsoring the NextGen Accelerator Program, a 2-week #startup bootcamp focused on early-stage student #founders from Historically Black Colleges and Universities (#HBCUs), and universities in Sub-Saharan Africa. I was lucky enough to meet with the cohort today. I cannot begin to express how impressed I am with this group of #entrepreneurs. I wasn’t 30 seconds in to my presentation (#ChalkTalk!) before the hands started popping up. And it went from there. Super engaged, super inquisitive, super smart. On the business side, I shared the Pacaso story as a way to highlight concepts like leveraging relationships, risk mitigation, ideation, #TAM, #PMF, B2C v B2B, navigating challenges, taking advantage of unexpected opportunities, scaling and, of course, #fundraising. With some time on “entrepreneurship as a profession.” Austin Allison and Spencer Rascoff, I am sure your ears were burning… On the legal side, we transitioned from the fundraising conversation into entity selection, #QSBS, founders stock, vesting, voting, cap tables, #dilution, #valuation, #seeddeals, syndication, exits, and more. Such an honor and a privilege to share the afternoon with this group. Thank you for the invite, Leo Guyshan. Always great partnering with you! Can’t wait to cheer this powerhouse group on, and to track their progress. I have no doubt they’ll succeed. #DWProf
